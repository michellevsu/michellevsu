New Product Launch Plan

interTrend Communications

In a Nutshell
  • Consulted with fast growth men's skincare brand to create new product launch plan targeting millennial men.
  • Started from conducting secondary research, focus groups and surveys, and data analysis.
  • End deliverables included detailed consumer personas, creative brief, concepting mockups, and final 3-pronged launch plan timeline.

To protect client anonymity, all future reference of this product and brand will be labeled as "Client Brand*."

UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM

I, along with a group of 12 other interns, were tasked with creating a new product launch plan for an extension product line of a men's skincare brand. To do this, we had to determine and target key drivers of millennial males' skincare product purchases.

After initial secondary research, we determined a set of hypotheses to test. These hypotheses guided the creation of our survey (sample size: 200) and focus groups (2 rounds, 12 people).

Survey and Focus Groups

ANALYZING DATA

After 10 days of conducting research, we began analyzing survey results and reconciling our secondary research and quantitative data with our qualitative data from focus groups. Using Excel Pivot Tables and Tableau data visualization, we determined important statistics on men's purchasing behaviors.

Key drivers came from testing correlation between different variables in our survey.

Ideating and Reiterating

The next step after determining key takeaways was ideating and iterating: while cross-referencing our research, we developed target consumer personas, a creative brief, and concepting for brand strategies.

PRESENTING RECOMMENDATIONS

Final Client Presentation

Upon deciding on the best creative brief and messaging strategy, we began developing a comprehensive launch plan. This included synthesizing key takeaways, performing feasibility analysis and creating budgets for strategy recommendations, and prioritizing short-term and long-term action plans.

Digital Marketing Strategies in Asia's Luxury Goods Industry

Richemont Asia Pacific Limited, IWC Schaffhausen

In a Nutshell
  • Researched digital marketing trends for luxury brands in Asia Pacific region.
  • Conducted competitor analysis among various luxury brands' social media strategies.
  • End deliverables included competitive analysis, social media trend report, and digital marketing strategy recommendations.

UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM

The objective of this summer internship was to determine industry-trending social media practices to recommend for IWC Schaffhausen Asia Pacific's branches. A secondary objective of this project was to specifically look at social media tactics to engage and capture more female customers.

In the first week, I conducted research to establish a better understanding of the luxury goods industry, specifically within the jewelry and accessories market in Asia. This included competitor analysis of social media presence and target audience of each brand.

A key takeaway in this research was that luxury goods are often purchased as part of attaining an aspired lifestyle, and as such, social media should be used used to curate this desired lifestyle image.

ANALYZING DATA

Understanding that social media strategy is extremely important for luxury goods to stay relevant to new audiences, I then collected data and conducted analysis of competitors' social media accounts.

Instagram user engagement competitive analysis, organized from most to least likes.

In addition to looking at competitors' followers and engagement, I determined categories of content and analyzed the breakdown of content posted by type. In this example, luxury brands' YouTube accounts mostly followed traditional methods of reposting TV advertisements. Here, the opportunity for IWC was to capitalize on this and use social media video outlets to engage users in more creative ways.

PRESENTING RECOMMENDATIONS

After conducting secondary research, employee interviews, and watch dealership/boutique site visits, I recommended various platform-specific social media strategies using case studies of competitors' campaigns.

Since IWC has tradtionally been an older, male-focused brand, much of my recommendations pointed out the ineffectiveness of current digital marketing strategies and outlined industry trends.

R.I.S.E. Advisory Case Competition

Deloitte Advisory

In a Nutshell
  • Collaborated with 3 other students in developing strategies for Deloitte's Advisory Case Competition in Spring 2017.
  • Identified risks and growth drivers relating to geographic expansion and regulatory & compliance issues to create a comprehensive response to a Request for Proposal.

UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM

Tasked with outlining key risks for multinational power tools manufacuturing company, Handy DIY, as they expanded into European region. Main issues involved finding a way to best monitor expenses of travel reimbursement costs and finding digital solutions that could translate across European offices.

Request for Proposal asked for recommendations on a Risk & Compliance Program, Internal Control Program, Compliance Monitoring Program, and a detailed resourcing mix, project rates, and timeline.

ANALYSIS

Researched and determined that main risks of company's proposed expansion included unpredictable global economic conditions, political landscapes, currency exchange rates, and market costs, among others.

Other risks we researched and determined included those of the client's current expense monitoring process, most of which were a result of potential human error.

A large majority of the research for this response to RFP was looking into Deloitte's Advisory Service lines and determining the best services and strategies to offer to the client.

PRESENTING RECOMMENDATIONS

Final presentation included resourcing mix strategy recommendation, proposed rates, and timeline to outline Deloitte Advisory's potential services to the client.

Proposed resourcing mix for client.

Service line recommendations and timeline of action items and deliverables due.

Estimated rates based on data provided by the case.
